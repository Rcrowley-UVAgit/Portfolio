Projects

  • LegalRAG: Trademark Infringement Analysis with Triplet Loss Optimization

    The legal right to protect a trademark from infringement depends primarily on whether a mark is categorized as “generic, descriptive, suggestive, arbitrary, or fanciful,” based on the relationship between the mark and the goods or services it identifies. This paper introduces a novel Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline designed to predict distinctiveness between trademarks, the foundational step in any infringement claim. The embedding model is a triplet network of SentenceBERT encoders trained on anchor, positive, and negative product-mark pairs. This framework shifts the trademark infringement prediction question from a computer-vision based paradigm to a natural language one. different than existing computer-vision based detection.

  • Automated 13F Filing Prototype

    This React prototype turns Form 13F preparation into a validation workflow. It separates reportable long positions from positions that do not belong in the filing, including shorts. It checks CUSIPs, issuer names, security classes, fair values, option put-call fields, aggregation rules, and amendment issues. It also records each correction the user applies. The app distinguishes U.S.-listed foreign issuers that appear on the SEC Official 13(f) Securities List from positions that should stay out of the EDGAR submission. After validation, it generates Form 13F-HR/A-oriented XML and information-table data so the user can see how the corrected holdings would flow into a filing.

  • Watchdogs: FDA Inspection Geolocation Signal

    This project adapts the research design from “Watching the Watchdogs” to FDA inspections. The basic question is whether FDA investigator visits can reveal approval or enforcement risk before the market sees a public disclosure. The relevant disclosure could be inspection observations, remediation demands, a delayed approval, a Warning Letter, or a Complete Response Letter. The signal is not simply more people visiting a building. A usable system would need to connect public companies to drug candidates, manufacturing sites, PDUFA dates, and facility risk history. It would also need to distinguish FDA inspection traffic from employees, vendors, contractors, nearby points of interest, and ordinary contract-manufacturer activity.

  • Navigating the Evolving Australian Tax Regime for U.S. Cloud Multinationals

    This client memorandum analyzes the Australian Taxation Office’s 2024 audit program for U.S. cloud multinationals. The target structure routes Asia-Pacific revenue through Singapore while using Australian data-center infrastructure. The main risk is permanent establishment treatment under the Australia-Singapore treaty’s “substantial equipment” clause. That clause may let the ATO argue that Australian servers are a core part of the cloud business, not merely auxiliary support. The memorandum also evaluates royalty withholding and Diverted Profits Tax theories after PepsiCo. It recommends treaty-routing review, infrastructure restructuring, sovereignty-defense files tied to Australian data-residency rules, contract cleanup around service fees, and transfer-pricing adjustments.
